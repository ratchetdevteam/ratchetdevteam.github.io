The CRAG

Since before the rise of mankind’s dominion, celestial giants have fallen from the sky, causing death and destruction in their wake, yet bringing power and life to those strong enough to challenge their might. Their existence has shaped the world as we know it, for better or for worse.

Our story takes place in the land of Cidire, an isolated continent, surrounded by treacherous seas. The peoples of this world, dependent on the Crag for magical energy and resources, grew from nothing into sentient beings, powerful in their own right. After the demise of the first major established stone age society, the Maedorran Empire, Cidire is currently undergoing a period of turmoil and transition.

The Factions

The fall and subsequent fragmentation of the Maedorran Empire has destroyed alliances and splintered the land of Cidire into a myriad of factions and minor kingdoms. Vying for the remaining land and power are six major contenders.

Maedorra

The first great civilization to emerge on the continent of Cidire many millennia ago. Its people believe in the superiority of the unadulterated Maedorran bloodline, spurning those of mixed or unknown parentage. Maedorrans were the ones who began the everlasting hunt for the power of the Crag. Their achievements established mankind as a dominant force in Cidire; their cities became the basis of all future societies and kingdoms. The decline of the Maedorran Empire coincided with the demise of the primordial Crag, and its research and knowledge has since been lost to the ages. Maedorra is a ghost of a kingdom, its people lost to the southern jungle.

Nivasa

A vast city-state that sits on the desolate plateau of Sen’abo, Nivasa is constantly expanding to keep its reputation as a beacon of prosperity and hope in the wasteland. Its borders push against the northern forest of Ilium-Atall, where barbaric tribes still raid and pillage. Nivasa, ancient in origin, was established more than a thousand years ago by the last Empress of the Maedorran Empire, who founded the city with her supporters after being banished for opposing her husband’s oppressive reign. Its current king is obsessed with the power of the Crag.

Ilium-Atall

Ilium-Atall has only begun to reform its cultural identity. Its roots are found in a primitive pact between mankind and a Crag of great power to protect the forest of Atall. Originally, the tribes of Ilium-Atall were unified under a Maedorran priest called Ainor. Atall became a sanctuary to those fleeing the crumbling Maedorran empire, a place where no harm would come to those who entered in peace. However, after Ainor’s death, the Atallans began quarreling among themselves, fighting for land and resources and separating into distinct tribes. King Halvar currently reigns, the first to unite the forest kingdom in recent memory.

Atlas

The Atlas Company is comprised of a triad of enormous seafaring trading operations. Atlas controls the seas and regulate trade with an iron fist. Atlassian holdings and established trading posts can be found in the most remote parts of the world. The Atlas Company began as a simple trading business at the very beginning of the Maedorran Empire, but grew to be a major naval force after establishing a symbiotic partnership with the monstrous Zoa of the sea. Eventually, the Atlas Company severed links with ailing Maedorra and established themselves as a powerful kingdom in their own right.

Zoa

A sprawling collective hive mind that resides in the ocean and controls more species of creatures than mankind will ever observe. The existence of the Zoa dates back to before the start of the Maedorran empire, long before any recorded history. The mysterious power that dictates the Zoa’s every move has never been seen, its desires instead conveyed by its devoted subjects. They pillage anyone who enters their watery domain, with the exception of the Atlas Company.

O’Nai

A nationalistic plutocracy on the cusp of an industrial revolution. Powerful barons use their wealth and influence to push world events in their favor, with little regard for consequences. The O’nai Federation originated several hundred years ago from a group of mining colonies in the Naian Desert, established by the Atlas Company. O’nai was the Atlassian center for manufacturing and production until, about four hundred years ago, major industry leaders broke away from the Atlas Company and formed their own government.
